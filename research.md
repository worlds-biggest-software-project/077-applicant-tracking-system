## Relevant Industry Standards or Protocols

- **HR-XML / HR Open Standards Recruiting** — Data interchange formats for job requisitions, candidate applications, and interview feedback between ATS and downstream HR systems.
- **OFCCP / EEOC Compliance (US)** — Federal contractor affirmative action and equal employment opportunity recordkeeping requirements; ATS must maintain compliant audit trails for all applicants.
- **GDPR Article 22** — Right not to be subject to solely automated decision-making; governs use of AI screening scores in hiring; requires human oversight mechanisms and explainability.
- **NYC Local Law 144 (2023)** — First US law requiring bias audits for AI tools used in hiring in New York City; sets precedent for AI ATS regulation spreading to other jurisdictions.
- **WCAG 2.1 AA** — Web accessibility standard; career site and application portals must be accessible to candidates with disabilities.
- **LinkedIn Apply / Indeed Apply APIs** — De facto integration standards enabling one-click application from job boards; mandatory for competitive ATS adoption.
- **POPIA (South Africa) / PDPA (Thailand)** — Regional data protection laws governing applicant data; relevant for global ATS deployments.

## AI-Native Opportunity

- **Bias-audited, explainable AI screening:** SmartRecruiters' Winston Match and Workday/Paradox's Olivia demonstrate that AI screening can cut manual review by 75%. However, NYC Local Law 144 and pending EU AI Act requirements demand bias audits and explainability. An AI-native ATS built from the ground up with auditable scoring (not bolted-on AI) and published bias test results would be the first platform to use compliance as a differentiator rather than a liability.
- **Async AI interview with structured scoring:** Current async video tools (HireVue, Spark Hire) apply AI scoring post-recording. An AI-native ATS can conduct a structured, adaptive async interview via voice/text, score responses against the job scorecard in real time, and produce a ranked shortlist before a human recruiter logs in — compressing the screening step from days to minutes for roles receiving 500+ applications.
- **Intelligent job description optimization:** Job postings written by hiring managers consistently underperform because of jargon, bias-coded language, and missing keywords. AI trained on application conversion data and external labor market signals can rewrite, score, and A/B test job descriptions automatically, improving qualified applicant conversion rates.
- **Passive candidate relationship management at scale:** ATS CRM modules (Lever, Greenhouse) require manual sequencing of outreach. AI can analyze a recruiter's historical placement data, LinkedIn engagement signals, and role requirements to identify and prioritize passive candidates and generate personalized outreach — essentially automating the sourcing workflow.
- **OSS differentiation:** No serious open-source ATS exists for the mid-market (OpenCATS and Candid are minimally maintained). An open-source ATS with a modern React UI, AI screening, OFCCP/GDPR compliance hooks, and a structured interview scorecard system would address the substantial market of companies priced out of Greenhouse ($12K+/yr) or unwilling to lock into Workday's ecosystem — while the AI screening layer monetizes as a premium hosted service.
